## Meaning

Day and night stand exactly equal, and after today the light wins. It is the oldest new year on Earth — Babylonians, Persians, and Romans all began their year about now, when the ground softens and things start again.

## Observance

Plant something — seeds indoors, a tree outdoors, whatever the season allows. Balance an egg on its end if you like the old trick. Open every window and let the house exhale the winter.

## Reading

> "In the spring, at the end of the day, you should smell like dirt."
> — Margaret Atwood

Day and night stand exactly equal, and after today the light wins. It is the oldest new year on Earth — Babylon, Persia, and Rome all began their year about now — and its business is the honest, hands-in-the-soil business of beginnings. Atwood's line is the whole of it: spring is not an abstraction to admire through a window but a thing you are meant to get your hands dirty in.
