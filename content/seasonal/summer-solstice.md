## Meaning

The longest day of the year, the sun at the very top of its arc. From here the days shorten again, so the light is worth spending while we have this much of it. This is the year at full flood.

## Observance

Be outside from before sunrise to after sunset if you can. Eat dinner outdoors. Watch the sun go down together and don't rush it — it's the latest it will set all year.

## Reading

> I am he that walks with the tender and growing night,
> I call to the earth and sea half-held by the night.
> Press close bare-bosom'd night—press close magnetic nourishing night!
> Night of south winds—night of the large few stars!
> Still nodding night—mad naked summer night.
> Smile O voluptuous cool-breath'd earth!
> Earth of the slumbering and liquid trees!
> — Walt Whitman, "Song of Myself," 21 (Leaves of Grass, 1892)
