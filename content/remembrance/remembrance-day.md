## Meaning

At the eleventh hour of the eleventh day of the eleventh month, 1918, the guns of the First World War fell silent, and a generation counted its dead. The day has since come to hold all the war dead — of every war, every side, every uniform and none. The red poppy grew in the churned fields of Flanders where so many were buried; we wear it to say that the peace we live in was paid for by people who did not get to keep it.

## Observance

Keep the two minutes' silence at the eleventh hour if you can. Wear a poppy. Read the roll of anyone in the family who served or fell. Speak of the cost of war honestly — not glory, but grief — so the children learn to want peace fiercely.
