## Meaning

On this day in 1945 the gates of Auschwitz were opened, and the full scale of the Holocaust began to be counted: six million Jews, and millions of others, murdered by an ordinary modern nation that had decided some people were not people. The horror was not done by monsters from elsewhere; it was done by neighbors, clerks, and soldiers who went along. 'Never again' is not a memory. It is a duty — the daily refusal to let anyone be turned into less than human.

## Observance

The gravest day of the year. Light candles — six, by tradition — and keep silence. Read one name, one story, so the number becomes a person. Speak plainly with the children about how it happened, and about the small, early refusals — to mock, to exclude, to look away — that are how ordinary people stop it happening again.
