## Meaning

Around 1455, in Mainz, Johannes Gutenberg printed a Bible with movable metal type — and quietly detonated the old world. Before him, every book was copied by hand, slow and rare and expensive, and knowledge belonged to the few who could afford it. After him, ideas could be copied by the thousand and could no longer be contained. Printing lit the fuse under the Reformation, the Scientific Revolution, and mass literacy itself. Almost every good thing on this calendar that depends on shared knowledge traces back through this one machine.

## Observance

A day to appreciate the humble, world-shaking act of copying and sharing what we know. Read a physical book together. Talk about how a single technology for spreading ideas cheaply can change everything downstream — and about the internet as the latest chapter of the same story.

## Reading

> "Printing, gunpowder, and the compass: these three have changed the whole face and state of things throughout the world."
> — Francis Bacon, Novum Organum

Of the three, printing may be the one that changed us most — because it changed what everyone else could know.
