## Meaning

The spring block is about changing your mind and escaping the tribe, and this is where it starts: most of what we think, we never actually thought. We cached it. Somebody said it once, it sounded right, and the brain filed it away and now serves it back up on demand without ever re-running the computation. The danger is that a cached thought feels exactly like a fresh conclusion, so we defend inherited slogans as though we had earned them. The remedy is not cynicism but maintenance — noticing which of your ideas you have only ever downloaded, and reopening a few of them.

## Observance

Each person names one belief they hold that they are pretty sure they absorbed from someone else — a parent, a church, a friend, the internet — rather than reasoned out. You do not have to reject it. Just say it out loud, notice where it came from, and ask together whether it would survive being thought a second time.
