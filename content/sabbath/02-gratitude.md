## Meaning

Attention drifts toward what's wrong — it's how we were built, and it kept our ancestors alive. Gratitude is the deliberate correction: once a week, turning the same attention on what went right, what held, who showed up. Nothing supernatural is required to be genuinely thankful; the luck of being here at all is more than enough to be astonished by.

## Observance

Go around the table and each name one thing from the week you're grateful for — small is fine, small is better. Write the best ones somewhere they accumulate: a jar, a notebook, the back page of the calendar. At year's end you'll have a record of a life that was, on balance, good.

## Reading

> Gratitude is not only the greatest of the virtues, but the parent of all the others.
> — Cicero, *Pro Plancio*

To be grateful is already to have noticed that you were given something — and noticing is the whole of it.
