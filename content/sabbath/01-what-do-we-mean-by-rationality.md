## Meaning

Before a season of clear thinking, it helps to agree on what the word even means. Yudkowsky offers two crisp definitions: epistemic rationality is systematically improving the accuracy of your beliefs, and instrumental rationality is systematically achieving your values. One is about seeing the world as it is; the other is about getting what you want. Keeping them separate saves a great deal of confusion, because sometimes the true belief and the useful action pull in different directions. This is the map for the whole winter block, so it earns the first Sabbath.

## Observance

Read the two definitions aloud and let each person say which one they lean on more in daily life. Then pick one small belief and one small goal from the past week, and name which kind of rationality each belonged to. No grading, just sorting.

## Reading

> 1. Epistemic rationality: systematically improving the accuracy of your beliefs.
> 2. Instrumental rationality: systematically achieving your values.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "What Do We Mean By 'Rationality'?"

The rest of the winter block is mostly about the first kind: building a more accurate map.
