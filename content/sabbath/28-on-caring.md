## Meaning

Soares names a thing most of us feel but rarely say: our internal sense of caring is a broken instrument for the scale of the world's problems. One death and ten thousand deaths produce nearly the same flicker in the chest, because the feeling was never built to multiply. The mistake is to conclude that we therefore do not really care. Caring, he argues, is something you can choose to do with your actions even when you cannot make yourself feel it in proportion — and that deliberate caring is the only kind big enough to matter.

## Observance

Read it together, then admit out loud that the numbers do not move you the way a single sad face does — and that this is normal, not a defect of character. Pick one thing this week you will do on purpose because it matters, not because it tugs at you. Let the doing stand in for the feeling.

## Reading

> Our care-o-meters are broken. They don't work on large numbers. Nobody has one capable of faithfully representing the scope of the world's problems. But the fact that you can't feel the caring doesn't mean that you can't do the caring.
> — Nate Soares, "On Caring"

The instrument is broken; the work is still real.
