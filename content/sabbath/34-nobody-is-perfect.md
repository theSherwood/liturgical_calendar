## Meaning

After a season of demanding essays, this one is the exhale. Alexander notices that the logic of doing good, taken to its limit, can crush you: if more is always better, then nothing you do is ever enough, and the guilt curdles into doing nothing at all. His escape hatch is deliberately unheroic — set an arbitrary, achievable goal, like giving ten percent, and let hitting it be genuinely good rather than merely insufficient. Nobody is perfect, and that fact is not an accusation; it is a permission slip to do a real, bounded amount of good and feel fine about it.

## Observance

Read it together and each pick a number you can actually live with — of money, of time, of effort — and say it out loud so it counts. The point is to choose a line you can stand on without flinching, then treat crossing it as a win, not a floor to feel bad about. Ritualize the exhale: enough is a thing you are allowed to reach.

## Reading

> Nobody is perfect. This gives us license not to be perfect either. Instead of aiming for an impossible goal, falling short, and not doing anything at all, we set an arbitrary but achievable goal designed to encourage the most people to do as much as possible.
> — Scott Alexander, "Nobody Is Perfect, Everything Is Commensurable"
