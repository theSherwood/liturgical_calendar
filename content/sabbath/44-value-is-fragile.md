## Meaning

This is the hinge of the season, the essay that turns the study of value into a matter of urgency. Yudkowsky argues that the specific things we cherish — humor, love, curiosity, the whole godshattered inheritance — are a tiny, particular point in the vast space of ways a future could be arranged. Remove almost any piece of what we value, and you do not get a slightly worse world; you get a world with nearly nothing worth having, optimized brilliantly toward something we would not recognize as good. The future does not come pre-loaded with our values. If they are to exist out there, we have to reliably hand them forward.

## Observance

Read it together in a quieter voice than usual. Then name one value you would want a distant descendant, or a mind we someday build, to still hold a million years from now, and say why losing it would empty the world rather than merely dim it. Let the weight of that sit before moving on.

## Reading

> Any Future not shaped by a goal system with detailed reliable inheritance from human morals and metamorals will contain almost nothing of worth.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Value is Fragile"
