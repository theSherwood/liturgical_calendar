## Meaning

Utilitarianism gets caricatured as a cold spreadsheet, but this essay argues the opposite: the impulse to add things up is itself a moral intuition, and often a more trustworthy one than the gut reactions we flatter as deep wisdom. Yudkowsky's case is that our snap judgments run on evolved, error-prone hardware — great for small human situations, unreliable at scale — and that "shut up and multiply" is not the abandonment of feeling but its correction. The point is humility about our own intuitions, not contempt for them. When the stakes are numerical, refusing to count is a choice too, just a worse one.

## Observance

Read it together and each name one moral intuition you hold strongly. Then ask, gently, what it would predict if you followed it all the way out — does it still look right at a thousand times the scale? Let multiplication be a tool you point at your own certainties first.
