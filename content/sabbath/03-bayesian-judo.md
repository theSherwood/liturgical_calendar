## Meaning

A short, funny one — the anecdote of an argument about whether a sufficiently powerful AI could talk its way out of a locked box, and a theologian who held that faith was beyond all reason. The point underneath the joke is the same lever as the whole plan: if a claim is really true, it has to cash out somewhere, in some difference you could point to. A belief that touches nothing is doing no work.

## Observance

Keep this one light. Read it, laugh, and let someone play devil's advocate at the table — then ask them, kindly, what their position would predict if they're right. Argument as a game you play together toward the truth, not against each other.

## Reading

A brief, self-contained tale; read it in full at the source. Its moral, stripped of the story: *if it's real, it makes a difference somewhere — so go and find the difference.*
