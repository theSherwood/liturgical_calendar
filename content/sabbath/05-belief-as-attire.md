## Meaning

Beliefs can be clothing: worn to signal membership more than to track the world. When an opinion becomes part of the uniform of a group we love, it gets very hard to take off — disagreeing starts to feel like betraying the people, not just revising a claim. Seeing this clearly is a kindness we do ourselves, so that we can change our minds without feeling we've changed our tribe.

## Observance

Read it, then ask lightly: which of our views are also our wardrobe — things we'd feel oddly disloyal setting down? No need to take any of them off tonight. It's enough to see which ones are stitched to belonging.

## Reading

Read the full essay at the source. Hold onto its central image: that some beliefs are worn, not held — and that a person can love their people wholeheartedly while still keeping their map of the world their own.
