## Meaning

This is a shaggy, funny parable about a shepherd who counts sheep by dropping pebbles in a bucket, and a visitor who insists truth is just whatever you believe. The bucket works because reality, not opinion, controls how many pebbles are inside. That is the whole point: a belief is true when the world has been allowed to shape it, rather than the other way around. It is the friendliest possible introduction to correspondence — the idea that truth is a match between the map in your head and the territory outside it. Read as a story first, and the philosophy sneaks in behind it.

## Observance

Read the pebble-and-bucket scenes together, doing the voices if you like. Then ask: what is a small "bucket" in our house — a checklist, a thermometer, a bank balance — that stays honest only because reality keeps controlling it?

## Reading

> The correspondence between reality and my beliefs comes from reality controlling my beliefs, not the other way around.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "The Simple Truth"
