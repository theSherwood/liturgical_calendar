## Meaning

A belief earns its keep only by changing what you expect to see. If two people say they disagree but anticipate exactly the same experiences, they are not really disagreeing about the world; they are arguing about labels. The test of a real belief is that it sticks its neck out and forecasts something you could actually observe. This is the practical heart of epistemic rationality, and a good rung for a family that wants to think clearly together.

## Observance

Read it aloud, then each person names one thing they believe and says plainly what they expect to see because it is true, and what they would expect if it were false. If nothing changes either way, notice that gently and enjoy the discovery.

## Reading

> Above all, don't ask what to believe—ask what to anticipate. Every question of belief should flow from a question of anticipation, and that question of anticipation should be the center of the inquiry.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Making Beliefs Pay Rent (in Anticipated Experiences)"
