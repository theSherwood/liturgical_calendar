## Meaning

The oldest idea in the calendar is that the week should have a seam in it — a day when the doing stops and we remember we are not machines. We keep it without the theology: not because rest is commanded, but because a week that never pauses forgets its own point. To stop on purpose is to declare that our worth isn't the day's output. The work will still be there tomorrow; today we set it down.

## Observance

Do less, on purpose. No chores that can wait, no screens that only demand. Make it slow — a long breakfast, a walk, a nap in the afternoon light. Light a candle at the start to mark that the day is different, and let it burn down.

## Reading

> And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it.
> — Genesis 2:2–3 (KJV)

We don't need the Creator to keep the rest. What the old story got right is that even the maker of everything built a stopping-place into the week — and called it good.
