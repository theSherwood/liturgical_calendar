## Meaning

When we leave a group we used to belong to, there is a strong pull to believe the reverse of whatever it taught. If they were fools, surely the opposite of what they said is true. This reading gently dismantles that instinct: being wrong is easy, but being reliably, precisely wrong is nearly as hard as being right, so you cannot get truth for free just by flipping a fool. Your old tribe, or your enemies, will occasionally be correct — sometimes by accident, sometimes because they were right all along. Escaping a group means thinking for yourself, not just running its beliefs in reverse.

## Observance

Name something your former group, or a group you dislike, actually gets right. Say it plainly and without the softening "but." Notice whether it stings, and talk together about why agreeing with the wrong people can feel more threatening than being wrong yourself.

## Reading

> The world's greatest fool may say the Sun is shining, but that doesn't make it dark out.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Reversed Stupidity Is Not Intelligence"
