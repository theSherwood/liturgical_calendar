## Meaning

Occam's Razor is usually mangled into "the simplest explanation is the best," which invites Heinlein's mischievous reply that the simplest sentence is "the lady down the street is a witch; she did it." Yudkowsky's fix is that simplicity is not about how short the English is, but about how much you have to specify to actually reproduce what you saw. "Witchcraft did it" is short only because it quietly leaves out all the detail that still needs explaining. A genuinely simple hypothesis is one that lets you compress the data, not just wave at it. It is a fun, sharp correction to a proverb everyone thinks they already understand.

## Observance

Read the witch line aloud — it always gets a laugh. Then take a "simple" explanation someone offers this week and ask: does it actually let us predict the details, or does it just relabel them? Reward the explanation that does more work with fewer moving parts.
