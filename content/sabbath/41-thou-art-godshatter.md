## Meaning

Evolution optimized us for a single crude target, roughly "have more surviving grandchildren," and yet here we are wanting music and justice and the taste of salt and the company of friends, most of it disconnected from that original aim. The essay's beautiful move is to notice that this is not a bug but the whole miracle: a blind process aiming at one thing shattered into beings who cherish many things, and now pursue them for their own sake. We are, in that sense, the fragments a mindless optimizer flung off, glittering with values it never intended. That inheritance is exactly why our values are ours and not evolution's.

## Observance

Read it aloud, then go around and each name one thing you love that is gloriously useless for survival, the more useless the better. Sit for a moment with the strangeness that a process about grandchildren produced a creature that weeps at a chord change. Let that be the week's small astonishment.
