## Meaning

The people we love are the ones easiest to take for granted — always there, always tomorrow. A weekly day of connection is insurance against that drift: a fixed time when the family turns toward each other, and reaches out past the household to the friends and kin who've slipped just out of orbit. Presence is the whole gift; it only counts if you actually show up.

## Observance

Eat together, unhurried and unscreened. Then reach one person outside the house — a call, not a text, to a grandparent, a cousin, an old friend. Let the children hear you do it; that's how they learn a family stays a family on purpose.

## Reading

> Two are better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour. For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth; for he hath not another to help him up.
> — Ecclesiastes 4:9–10 (KJV)

The oldest practical wisdom there is: alone is more fragile than together. The week is the labour; this is the good reward.
