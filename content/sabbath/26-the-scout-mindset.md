## Meaning

Julia Galef closes the spring block with the metaphor that ties it all together. A soldier's job is to defend and attack — to hold the line for the belief they already have. A scout's job is different: to go out, look honestly, and draw an accurate map, even when the map brings unwelcome news. Most of us default to the soldier when our identity is on the line, treating every challenge as an assault to be repelled. The scout mindset is the deliberate choice to want the true picture more than the comforting one. It is not about being unemotional; it is about which feeling you let win when the evidence and your ego disagree.

## Observance

For each disagreement this week, ask one quiet question: am I being a soldier or a scout right now? Practice the scout's tell — a flash of curiosity, rather than defensiveness, when someone shows you might be wrong. Reward that flash in each other when you see it.

## Reading

Galef defines the scout mindset simply, as the disposition the whole season has been building toward:

> the motivation to see things as they are, not as you wish they were
> — Julia Galef, "The Scout Mindset"
