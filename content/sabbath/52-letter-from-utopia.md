## Meaning

The season closes not with an argument but with an invitation. Bostrom writes in the voice of a being you could one day become, or one your descendants could become, sending a letter back across the gap to tell you what is waiting: a mode of existence so rich in beauty and joy that present language strains to point at it. The whole autumn's caution — value is fragile, the future is not guaranteed, Moloch is real — was in service of protecting the possibility this letter describes. It is the answer to "what is worth wanting" given not as theory but as a hand reaching back toward you, asking you to help make it so.

## Observance

Read it aloud together, slowly, as the last Sabbath of the season. Then let each person say one thing they hope is true of a life like that, one good they would want the far future to hold. End in silence for a breath. You have spent a whole season learning what is worth wanting; this is the picture of it kept.

## Reading

> There is a beauty and joy here that you cannot fathom. It feels so good that if the sensation were translated into tears of gratitude, rivers would overflow.
> — Nick Bostrom, "Letter from Utopia"
