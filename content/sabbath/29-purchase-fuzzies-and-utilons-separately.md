## Meaning

A cheerful piece of practical wisdom hiding a sharp point. When you try to feel good and do good in the same act, you tend to shortchange both: you pick the thing that tugs your heart over the thing that helps most, and you feel warmly enough about it that you stop looking. Yudkowsky's fix is to split the accounts. Buy your warm fuzzies deliberately — volunteer, hold the door, bake the thing — and, entirely separately, spend where the actual good is greatest. Each does its job better once it is not pretending to be the other.

## Observance

This week, do one thing purely for the fuzzies — hands-on, local, heart-warming — and name it as such, guilt-free. Then, in a separate breath, make one decision aimed only at doing the most good, and let it be as unsentimental as it needs to be. Notice how much lighter both feel once they stop competing.
