## Meaning

This is the doorway into the whole autumn season, and it asks a simple question with a lifetime inside it: which of the things you want are means, and which are ends? Instrumental values are the ones you would trade away the instant they stopped delivering — money, a commute, a clever plan. Terminal values are the ones at the bottom, wanted for their own sake, the place the chain of "but why?" finally stops. Learning to tell them apart is how you notice when you have been chasing a means so long you forgot the end it was for.

## Observance

Read it together, then play the "but why" game. Someone names something they want this week, and the others gently ask "but why do you want that?" until the answers stop bottoming out in anything further. Notice what sits at the very bottom for each of you. Those roots are what the rest of the season is about.

## Reading

> "Terminal values" are desirable without conditioning on other consequences: "I want to save my sister's life" has nothing to do with your anticipating whether she'll get injected with penicillin after that.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Terminal Values and Instrumental Values"
