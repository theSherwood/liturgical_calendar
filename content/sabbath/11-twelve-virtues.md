## Meaning

This is the most openly liturgical text in the block: twelve named virtues, written in the cadence of scripture, ending in a nameless twelfth that is the whole point. Curiosity, relinquishment, lightness, evenness, argument, empiricism, simplicity, humility, perfectionism, precision, scholarship — and then the reminder that the virtues are only fingers pointing at the moon. It reads beautifully aloud, which is exactly why it belongs on a family Sabbath. A secular household can hold this the way another might hold a psalm: not as a rule to obey, but as words worth returning to. Winter closes soon, and this gives the season a spine.

## Observance

Read the twelve virtues aloud, slowly, taking turns by virtue. Then let each person claim the one they most want to practice this coming season, and say why in a sentence. Return to the list whenever the year feels shapeless.

## Reading

> The first virtue is curiosity. A burning itch to know is higher than a solemn vow to pursue truth.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "The Twelve Virtues of Rationality"
