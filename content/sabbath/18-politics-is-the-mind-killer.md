## Meaning

This is the heart of the spring theme, and the hardest reading in it. Politics recruits our reasoning into a war: as soon as we identify a side, arguments stop being tools for finding the truth and become soldiers fighting for our team. We start defending claims because they help our people and attacking claims because they help the other tribe, whether or not they are correct. The essay is not telling us to have no values; it is warning that the moment an issue becomes a badge of belonging, our capacity to think about it honestly quietly shuts off. Naming that mechanism is the first defense against it.

## Observance

For the length of one reading, practice separating a political claim from its team. Take an issue the family cares about and ask, without scoring points: what would actually be true if our side were wrong here? The exercise is uncomfortable on purpose. Sit with the discomfort rather than resolving it too quickly.

## Reading

> Politics is an extension of war by other means. Arguments are soldiers. Once you know which side you're on, you must support all arguments of that side, and attack all arguments that appear to favor the enemy side.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Politics is the Mind-Killer"
