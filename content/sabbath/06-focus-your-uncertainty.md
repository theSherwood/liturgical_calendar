## Meaning

If a belief pays rent in anticipation, then anticipation is a budget: you have a fixed amount of expectation to distribute over the ways tomorrow could go, and every bit you put on one outcome is a bit you take from another. Good thinking is spending that budget well — concentrating it where the evidence points, instead of smearing it thin so you're never quite wrong and never quite right.

## Observance

Read it together, then make it a game: pick something genuinely uncertain in the week ahead and each say, out loud, how likely you think it is — a real number. Write the guesses down. Next Sabbath, see who spent their uncertainty wisely. Being wrong out loud, cheerfully, is the whole practice.
