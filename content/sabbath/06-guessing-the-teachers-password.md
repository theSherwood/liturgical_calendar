## Meaning

In school we learn to produce the phrase the teacher wants — "light is a wave," "energy makes it move" — and mistake the password for understanding. Real knowledge is the kind that controls your anticipations: if you can plug the idea into the world and get out expectations about what you will actually see, the map is connected to the territory. If you cannot, what you have is verbal behavior wearing the costume of a belief. This one stings a little, because we have all guessed a password and felt smart doing it. Naming the habit is the first step past it.

## Observance

Read it aloud, then each pick something you "know" and try to cash it out as a prediction — a number, an outcome, something checkable. If all you can produce is the right-sounding sentence, you have found a password; laugh and go looking for the real thing.

## Reading

> If you aren't using the diffusion equation—putting in numbers and getting out results that control your anticipation of particular experiences—then the connection between map and territory is severed as though by a knife. What remains is not a belief, but a verbal behavior.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Guessing the Teacher's Password"
