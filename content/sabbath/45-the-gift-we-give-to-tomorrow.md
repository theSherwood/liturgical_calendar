## Meaning

After the austere warning that value is fragile, this essay is the season's warm exhale. It asks, in the form of a dialogue that reads almost like scripture, how a mindless cosmos and a blind process with no love in it managed to produce beings who love fiercely. The answer it circles toward is disarmingly gentle: love did not need a loving source. It was created by something that did not love, and now it perpetuates itself the only way it can, because those who love make new creatures and teach them to love in turn. The universe did not give us this. We give it to each other, and forward.

## Observance

Read it aloud together, ideally the closing catechism, trading lines around the table like a call and response. Then let each person finish the sentence "I first learned to love from..." Notice that the recursion runs backward through everyone who ever loved you, all the way down.

## Reading

> And your children's children will ask: But how is it that you love?
> And their parents will reply: Because our own parents, who also loved, created us to love in turn.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "The Gift We Give To Tomorrow"
