## Meaning

Here is the essay that inoculates you against the wireheading dream, the pill that would deliver the feeling of discovery without any discovery. The trap is thinking that because good things make us happy, happiness must be the only thing we really want. But most of us, offered pure guaranteed bliss detached from any real achievement or love or truth, feel something recoil. That recoil is the data: we care about the world beyond our own pleasure, and a life is not made good by feeling good alone.

## Observance

Read it together, then pose the machine question. If a device could give you the exact sensation of your proudest accomplishment, with no accomplishment behind it, would you plug in? Go around and answer honestly, and notice what each person is unwilling to give up.

## Reading

> The question, rather, is whether we should care about the things that make us happy, apart from any happiness they bring.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Not For the Sake of Happiness (Alone)"
