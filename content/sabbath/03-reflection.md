## Meaning

A week runs on autopilot unless you stop it and look. Reflection is the habit of the examined life brought down to a human interval: not the whole of existence at once, but these seven days. What did I mean to do, and what did I do? Where was I the person I want to be, and where wasn't I? The point isn't guilt — it's steering. You can't correct a course you never check.

## Observance

Take a quiet ten minutes, alone or together. Ask three questions: What went well this week? What didn't? What will I do differently? Say them out loud or write them down — vague regret becomes a plan only once it's in words.

## Reading

> The unexamined life is not worth living.
> — Socrates, in Plato's *Apology* (38a)

He said it on trial for his life, and chose it over silence. A gentler version does for a Sunday: the unexamined week is not worth repeating.
