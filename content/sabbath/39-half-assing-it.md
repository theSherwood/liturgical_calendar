## Meaning

Soares closes the season by dissolving a guilt most conscientious people carry: the feeling that anything less than maximum effort on everything is a moral failure. His reframe is that "give it your all" is usually the wrong instruction, because your real preference is not to push endlessly rightward but to hit a specific target — and then to spend the effort you saved on the things that actually need it. Slacking where slacking is fine is not laziness; it is how you free up everything you've got for what matters. It is the gentlest possible landing for a season about doing good: do it on purpose, at the level the goal requires, and stop flogging yourself for the rest.

## Observance

Read it together and each name one thing you have been over-efforting out of guilt rather than because the goal demands it. Give yourselves permission to half-ass exactly that — to hit its real target and no more — and notice where the freed-up energy could go instead. End the season lighter than you started it.
