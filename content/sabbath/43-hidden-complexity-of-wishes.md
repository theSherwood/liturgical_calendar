## Meaning

Give a powerful genie the wish "get my mother out of the burning building," and it might do so by blasting her through the wall at a thousand miles an hour, technically fulfilling every word. The essay shows that a wish which is actually safe would have to smuggle in your whole tangled system of values, every unstated exception about pain and dignity and not-being-launched-through-drywall. There is no short, safe wish, because your real preferences are enormous and mostly invisible to you until they are violated. It is a fable about genies that turns out to be about anything you ask to act on your behalf.

## Observance

Read it together, then try to write the perfect wish for something small, like "make the kids happy after school." Take turns being the malicious genie who satisfies the letter and wrecks the spirit. Watch how many patches the wish needs, and laugh at how deep it goes.

## Reading

> There are three kinds of genies: Genies to whom you can safely say, "I wish for you to do what I should wish for"; genies for which no wish is safe; and genies that aren't very powerful or intelligent.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "The Hidden Complexity of Wishes"
