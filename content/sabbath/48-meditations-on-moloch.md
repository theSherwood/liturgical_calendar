## Meaning

Scott Alexander takes an old name for the god of child sacrifice and repurposes it as the name for a modern horror: the way competition and bad incentives can force everyone, each acting rationally, to grind away the very things they care about. No villain is required. Farmers race to the bottom, industries burn the commons, arms races consume the future, all because whoever refuses to sacrifice gets outcompeted by whoever will. It is the dark twin of the season's hopeful pieces, explaining why a world full of people who want good things can still lurch toward outcomes nobody chose. Naming that force is the first move against it.

## Observance

Read at least the opening sections together, enough to meet Moloch. Then spot him in the wild: name one race-to-the-bottom you are caught in this week, some situation where everyone would be better off if they could all agree to stop but no one can move first. Ask what a real act of coordination would look like.
