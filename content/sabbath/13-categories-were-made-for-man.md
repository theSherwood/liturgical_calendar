## Meaning

Scott Alexander closes the winter block by turning the lens on the words themselves. Are whales fish? Is Pluto a planet? He argues that categories are not carved into the universe; we draw them to serve human purposes, and a boundary can be more or less useful without being true or false. This is not a license to believe anything — reality still constrains what a category can usefully predict — but it dissolves a whole class of pointless fights about what something "really is." After twelve weeks of sharpening the map, it is a graceful reminder that the map's very borders are things we choose. A warm, humane place to end the season.

## Observance

Read it together, then find a definitional argument your family keeps having — what counts as a sandwich, a sport, a real vacation. Instead of settling who is right, ask what each boundary is for. Let usefulness, not metaphysics, be the tiebreaker.

## Reading
