## Meaning

Not everything shaped like a belief is meant to describe reality. Some professions of belief are more like cheering at a game — a way of showing whose side you're on, of belonging. That's not always wrong, but it's worth being able to tell the difference: a flag you wave is a fine thing, so long as you don't mistake it for a map of the territory.

## Observance

Read it together, then a gentle round: name a "belief" that's really a cheer — a team, a tribe, a badge we're glad to wear. Waving the flag is human and good; the ritual is just noticing when we're doing it.

## Reading

Read the essay in full at the source, and sit for a moment afterward with the distinction it draws — between a statement that anticipates the world and one that announces where you belong. Both have their place; the skill is telling which is which.
