## Meaning

There is a quiet snobbery in treating money as grubby and time as noble, and this essay takes it apart. Money is fungible caring: it is the common currency into which almost any kind of help can be converted, which is exactly why it can go wherever the need is greatest instead of only where you happen to be standing. Yudkowsky's point is not that money is the only thing that matters, but that refusing to think about it clearly — preferring the warm inefficiency of donating your amateur time — is often a way of caring about your self-image more than the outcome. If you want to help, the least glamorous lever is frequently the strongest.

## Observance

Read it together, then do one honest sum: pick a cause you say you care about and ask whether an hour of your money would do more there than an hour of your hands. Let the answer be allowed to surprise you. If it points at giving, give a small amount this week and notice that it felt like caring, because it was.

## Reading

> In our society, this common currency of expected utilons is called "money." It is the measure of how much society cares about something.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Money: The Unit of Caring"
