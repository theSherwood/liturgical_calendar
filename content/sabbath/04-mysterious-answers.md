## Meaning

There is a kind of answer that sounds deep but does no work — vitalism explaining life by a "life force," a word that names the mystery instead of dissolving it. Yudkowsky's tell is that a mysterious answer stops your curiosity without letting you predict anything new: you feel satisfied, but you cannot say what you should now expect to see. The cure is to notice that mystery lives in the questioner, not in the thing being asked about. A star is not mysterious; our ignorance of it is. Learning to feel the difference is one of the most useful habits a clear thinker can build.

## Observance

Read it together, then hunt for a "mysterious answer" you have each accepted — a phrase that felt like an explanation but predicts nothing. Try to restate it as either a real prediction or an honest "we don't know yet."

## Reading

> Mystery is a property of questions, not answers.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "Mysterious Answers to Mysterious Questions"
