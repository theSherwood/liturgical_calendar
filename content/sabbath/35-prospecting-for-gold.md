## Meaning

Cotton-Barratt hands you the toolkit for the season's central question: given that you cannot fix everything, how do you find the spots where effort pays off most? His answer is a prospector's three questions — how much gold is here (importance), how easy is it to dig out (tractability), and how many others are already digging (neglectedness). Underneath sits a genuinely surprising fact about the world: opportunities to do good are heavy-tailed, so the best few are not a little better than average but wildly, orders-of-magnitude better. Searching well matters more than working hard.

## Observance

Read it together, then run the three questions on something your family already cares about: is it big, is it winnable, and is it crowded? You do not need real numbers — rough gestures are enough to feel how the framework sorts things. Treat it as a game of prospecting, not a verdict on anyone's cause.
