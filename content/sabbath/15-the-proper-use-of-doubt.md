## Meaning

Doubt has a reputation for being noble all by itself, as if the mere posture of uncertainty made you wise. This reading pushes back. A doubt is not a decoration you hang on a belief to look humble; it is a live question that should either destroy the belief or clear it. The people who most loudly profess their doubts are often the ones most careful never to follow a doubt all the way down, because a real one might cost them something. To doubt properly is to be willing to lose the argument you are having with yourself.

## Observance

Pick one belief in the household that someone genuinely half-doubts but has never resolved. Spend ten minutes actually chasing the doubt — what would settle it, what evidence would count, what you would do differently either way. The goal is not to win but to reach an ending: keep the belief with clear eyes, or set it down.

## Reading

> Every doubt exists to destroy itself, one way or the other. An unresolved doubt is a null-op; it does not turn the wheel, neither forward nor back.
> — Eliezer Yudkowsky, "The Proper Use of Doubt"
