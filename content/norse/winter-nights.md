## Meaning

The Norse marked the year in two halves, and Winter Nights was the doorway into the dark one — the harvest safely in, the animals brought down from the pastures, and a feast to honor the ancestors and the spirits of the land before the long cold. It's the northern cousin of Samhain and Thanksgiving both.

## Observance

A harvest-home meal from the pantry and the last of the garden. Raise a glass to the family who came before you, and say aloud that the year's work is done — the resting season has begun.
