## Meaning

The great Norse midwinter feast, kept over roughly twelve nights around the solstice — and the source of half our Christmas: the Yule log, the evergreen tree, the wassailing, the feasting in the teeth of the cold. It's the northern answer to the longest night: gather in, light a fire, and outlast the dark together.

## Observance

Burn a Yule log (or light a large candle to stand in for one) and keep some light going through the twelve nights. Bring an evergreen indoors. Feast, tell stories, and toast — 'skål!' — to absent friends and the returning sun.
