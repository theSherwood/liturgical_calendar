## Meaning

The other hinge of the Norse year: the first day of summer, when the seas opened and the season's ventures — voyages, plantings, undertakings — began. Sigrblót was a blessing on new beginnings and the courage to set out. Where Winter Nights says 'rest,' this one says 'go.'

## Observance

Name one venture — a project, a trip, a goal — that you're launching this warm season, and say it out loud to make it real. Toast to it. Then take the first small step today.
