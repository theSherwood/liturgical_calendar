## Meaning

After twenty years of campaigning by abolitionists — and centuries of unimaginable suffering — Britain outlawed the Atlantic slave trade on this day in 1807. It did not end slavery itself; that took another generation (1833), and the fight goes on against its modern forms. But it was the moment one of the world's great powers formally admitted that buying and selling human beings was a crime, and began, however slowly, to act like it. Moral progress is real, and it is made by people who refuse to accept 'that's just how the world is.'

## Observance

A day to honor the people who kept pushing when the cause looked hopeless — Equiano, Clarkson, Wilberforce, and the enslaved who resisted and testified. Talk about a wrong the world once shrugged at that we now find unthinkable, and ask what we shrug at today that our great-grandchildren will not forgive.
