## Meaning

On New Year's Day 1863, in the middle of a war that would kill more Americans than any other, Abraham Lincoln declared that the enslaved people of the rebelling states 'are, and henceforward shall be free.' It freed no one instantly and everyone eventually; it turned a war to preserve a union into a war to end slavery. Word did not reach the enslaved people of Texas until 19 June 1865 — the day now celebrated as Juneteenth. Freedom, like most good things, arrived by proclamation first and by long struggle after.

## Observance

A fitting weight to add to New Year's Day. Read a few lines of the Proclamation aloud. Talk about the gap between declaring something right and making it real — and about Juneteenth, the day the news finally arrived, as the completion of this one.
