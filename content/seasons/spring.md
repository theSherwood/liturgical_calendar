Spring is the world starting over. The ground softens, the light wins back the day, and the year finds its nerve again. It is the oldest new year on Earth — Babylon, Persia, and Rome all began the year about now — and its work is the work of beginnings: plant the seed, open the window, forgive the debt, set out on the venture you've been putting off. Everything here is about hope with its sleeves rolled up.
