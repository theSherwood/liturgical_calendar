Winter is the year at its lowest ebb — the longest nights, the hardest cold, the world stripped bare. So it is the season of gathering in: of fires and feasts and the people you love pulled close. Nearly every winter festival humanity keeps is, underneath, the same defiant act — lighting a flame in the dark and promising each other the sun comes back. From the solstice the light only grows.
