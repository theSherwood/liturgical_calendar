## Meaning

By the old calendar, Isaac Newton was born on Christmas Day — a happy coincidence the rationally-minded have adopted with delight. Newton took the same laws that pull an apple down and used them to weigh the Moon and chart the planets. 'Newtonmas' celebrates the audacious idea that the universe runs on knowable rules, and that a human mind can find them.

## Observance

Slip a little science into the Christmas warmth: drop an apple and talk about why it falls, or how the same force holds the Moon. Exchange a book. Toast 'Happy Newtonmas' alongside the season's other greetings.

## Reading

> Dr Sir, at the reading of your letter I was exceedingly pleased & satisfied with your generous freedom... What Des-Cartes did was a good step. You have added much several ways, & especially in taking the colours of thin plates into philosophical consideration. If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of Giants.
> — Isaac Newton, letter to Robert Hooke, Cambridge, 5 February 1675/6
