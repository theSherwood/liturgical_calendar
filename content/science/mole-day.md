## Meaning

Chemistry's answer to Pi Day, and proudly ridiculous. A 'mole' is how chemists count things too small to count — 6.02 × 10²³ of them, Avogadro's number, a figure so vast it borders on the absurd. That many grains of sand would bury the Earth. It is the bridge between the invisible world of atoms and the visible world of stuff you can weigh in your hand.

## Observance

Celebrate from 6:02 to 6:02 (a.m. to p.m., naturally). Make guacaMOLE. Try to picture 6.02 × 10²³ of anything and fail happily. Talk about how everything solid is mostly empty space and unimaginably small pieces.
