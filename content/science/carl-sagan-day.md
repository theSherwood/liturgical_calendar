## Meaning

The birthday of the scientist who, more than anyone, taught ordinary people to feel the scale and wonder of the cosmos — and to demand good evidence while they did it. Sagan married awe and skepticism so gracefully that neither had to lose. We are, as he said, a way for the universe to know itself.

## Observance

Watch a bit of Cosmos, or read the 'Pale Blue Dot' passage aloud under the night sky. Hold two things at once, the way Sagan did: boundless wonder, and a refusal to believe things without good reason.

## Reading

> Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us. On it everyone you love, everyone you know, everyone you ever heard of, every human being who ever was, lived out their lives... every saint and sinner in the history of our species lived there — on a mote of dust suspended in a sunbeam.
> — Carl Sagan, Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space (1994)

> To me, it underscores our responsibility to deal more kindly with one another, and to preserve and cherish the pale blue dot, the only home we've ever known.
> — Carl Sagan, Pale Blue Dot (1994)
