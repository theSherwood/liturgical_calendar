## Meaning

The cheeky rival to Pi Day, for the mathematicians who argue the true circle constant is τ = 2π ≈ 6.28, because a circle is really about its radius, not its diameter. Mostly it's an excuse to have the math argument again — and to eat twice as much pie as on Pi Day.

## Observance

Two pies, obviously — it's twice pi. Have the friendly family debate: is π or τ the more natural constant? There's no wrong answer, only a more fun one.
