## Meaning

A hundred years before a computer existed, Ada Lovelace looked at the plans for one and saw further than its own inventor. Where others saw a calculator, she saw that such a machine might one day manipulate not just numbers but any symbols at all — music, language, thought — and she wrote the first true algorithm meant to run on it. The day honors her, and all the women whose contributions to science were overlooked in their time.

## Observance

Learn about a woman in science whose name you don't know yet — there are more than any list admits. Try a little programming together. And notice who gets left out of the story, then put them back in.
