## Meaning

One of the oldest festivals ever recorded — the Babylonian New Year, kept at the first new moon after the spring equinox for over four thousand years. It was twelve days of renewal: debts forgiven, the world symbolically remade, even the king ritually humbled to remember he was only human. When we begin the year in spring, we are echoing Babylon.

## Observance

A festival of renewal and humility. Forgive one small debt or grudge. Put one thing back in order that's fallen into chaos. And, like the Babylonians did to their king, let whoever's proudest in the family be gently taken down a peg — in good humor.

## Reading

> — the Enūma Eliš, recited each year at Akitu
