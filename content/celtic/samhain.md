## Meaning

The ancient Celtic new year and the true root of Halloween — the night the year dies and the veil between the living and the dead was said to thin. Under the candy and costumes, this is our day for remembering the people we've lost.

## Observance

Set a place at the table, or light a candle, for someone in the family who has died. Tell a story about them — a funny one is best. Let the children hear the names of people they never met.

## Reading

> Midnight has come and the great Christ Church Bell,
> And many a lesser bell, sound through the room;
> And it is All Souls' Night,
> And two long glasses brimmed with muscatel
> Bubble upon the table. A ghost may come;
> For it is a ghost's right,
> His element is so fine
> Being sharpened by his death,
> To drink from the wine-breath
> While our gross palates drink from the whole wine.
> — W. B. Yeats, "All Souls' Night," from The Tower (1928)
