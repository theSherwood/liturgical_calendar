## Meaning

Britain's Mother's Day, older than the American one and pinned to the church calendar — the fourth Sunday of Lent. However it's dated, the point is identical: one guaranteed day to thank the mothers.

## Observance

Simnel cake or flowers by tradition; a made breakfast and a real thank-you in practice. The mothers rest; everyone else serves.

## Reading

> "All that I am, or hope to be, I owe to my angel mother."
> — Abraham Lincoln

Britain's Mother's Day, older than the American one and pinned to the church calendar — the fourth Sunday of Lent, when servants and apprentices were once released to travel home to their "mother church," and to their mothers. However it is dated, the point is the same and never dates: one guaranteed day to thank the least-thanked and most essential work there is. The mothers rest today; everyone else serves. Simnel cake and flowers by tradition; a made breakfast and a real, specific thank-you in practice.
