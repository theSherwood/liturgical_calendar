## Meaning

An arbitrary line drawn across the calendar — and none the worse for it. Human beings need a place to stop, look back, and begin again, and we've collectively agreed this is it. A fresh start is real precisely because we all decide to believe in it together.

## Observance

Each person writes down one thing to leave in the old year and one thing to carry into the new. Read them aloud or keep them secret. Do nothing productive; rest is the correct way to start.

## Reading

> Ring out, wild bells, to the wild sky,
> The flying cloud, the frosty light:
> The year is dying in the night;
> Ring out, wild bells, and let him die.
>
> Ring out the old, ring in the new,
> Ring, happy bells, across the snow:
> The year is going, let him go;
> Ring out the false, ring in the true.
>
> Ring out the grief that saps the mind
> For those that here we see no more;
> Ring out the feud of rich and poor,
> Ring in redress to all mankind.
> — Alfred, Lord Tennyson, "In Memoriam A. H. H." (1850), the section known as "Ring Out, Wild Bells"
