## Meaning

A holiday that honors labor by insisting everyone stop doing it — won by generations who fought for the weekend, the eight-hour day, and the idea that a life is more than a job. Rest is not the opposite of respecting work; it's the reward the work was for.

## Observance

Do nothing useful, on purpose. One last summer cookout, one last late evening outside before the year turns toward school and dark. Thank the people whose labor you rely on and rarely see.
