## Meaning

The day thirteen colonies declared that people could govern themselves — a radical, unfinished, still-arguing experiment in self-rule. We can love the idea while being honest that the country has always been a work in progress. That's the most patriotic thing there is.

## Observance

The full American summer ritual: grilling, being outside, and fireworks after dark. Somewhere in the day, read a line or two of the Declaration aloud — the good part about self-evident truths.

## Reading

> "We hold these truths to be self-evident, that all men are created equal…" — The Declaration of Independence, 1776
