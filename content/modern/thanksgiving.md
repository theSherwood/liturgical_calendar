## Meaning

The best of the American holidays, because it asks for nothing but presence and gratitude — no gifts, no fireworks, just a table full of people and food. Underneath its complicated history is a practice worth keeping: stopping once a year to say out loud what we're thankful for.

## Observance

Cook too much and eat together, but the real ritual is the go-around: before anyone eats, each person at the table names one thing they're genuinely grateful for this year. No skipping, no jokes for the little ones — say it and mean it.

## Reading

> "Gratitude is not only the greatest of virtues, but the parent of all the others."
> — Cicero

The best of the holidays, because it asks for nothing but presence and gratitude — no gifts, no fireworks, just a table full of people and food, and the quiet radical act of saying out loud what we're thankful for. Gratitude is not a feeling that happens to you; it is a practice, and this is the day we practice it together.
