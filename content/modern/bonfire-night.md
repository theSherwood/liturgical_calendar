## Meaning

Britain's great autumn fire night, marking the foiling of the 1605 plot to blow up Parliament. Four centuries on it's less about the politics and more about the primal pleasure of a huge fire and fireworks in the cold November dark.

## Observance

Fireworks and a bonfire if you can, sparklers if you can't. Toffee, warm hands, cold noses. Recite the old rhyme: 'Remember, remember, the fifth of November.'

## Reading

> "Remember, remember, the fifth of November, / gunpowder, treason and plot; / I see no reason why gunpowder treason / should ever be forgot."
> — traditional English rhyme
