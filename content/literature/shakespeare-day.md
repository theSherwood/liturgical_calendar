## Meaning

By tradition Shakespeare was born on 23 April 1564 and died on the same date in 1616, which gives the day a pleasing symmetry the plays themselves would enjoy. Cervantes died on that date too, so it has become a festival of literature in general — UNESCO keeps it as World Book Day. Shakespeare handed English thousands of its phrases and half its ways of feeling things, and did it all while running a theatre and turning a profit. This is a day for the strange power of arranged words. It costs nothing and lasts forever.

## Observance

Read something of his aloud after dinner — a sonnet, a soliloquy, or a scene passed around the table with everyone taking a part badly. Give or swap a book to mark World Book Day. Let someone under ten deliver a famous line with total conviction and no idea what it means.

## Reading
