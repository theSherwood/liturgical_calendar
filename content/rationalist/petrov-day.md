## Meaning

On this night in 1983, a Soviet early-warning system reported five American missiles inbound. The officer on duty, Stanislav Petrov, had minutes to decide whether to report it up the chain — which would likely have triggered nuclear retaliation and the end of the world as we know it. He judged it a false alarm. He was right. He received no reward, and for decades no one knew his name. Petrov Day honors the world-saving power of a calm, careful mind under unimaginable pressure — and the people who prevent catastrophes we never hear about because they prevented them.

## Observance

A solemn evening. Dim the lights and read Petrov's story aloud. Sit for a moment with how close it came, and how much rode on one person choosing to be careful rather than certain. Toast to restraint, to good judgment, and to everyone who quietly holds back disaster. Ask: where in our own lives should we slow down and check before we act?
