## Meaning

A feast day for the most underrated of virtues: changing your mind. Thomas Bayes, an 18th-century minister, left behind a theorem that is nothing less than the exact mathematics of learning — how much a new piece of evidence should shift what you believe. The rationalist community treats it almost as a creed: hold your beliefs in proportion to the evidence, and let them move when the evidence moves.

## Observance

Practice a real update. Each person names one thing they've changed their mind about this year, and what changed it — and updating is celebrated, never treated as losing. Bonus: find one belief you hold weakly and go look for evidence that would settle it.

## Reading

> "When the facts change, I change my mind. What do you do, sir?"
> — attributed to John Maynard Keynes

The rationalists borrow an old litany for days like this:

> If the box contains a diamond, I desire to believe that the box contains a diamond;
> If the box does not contain a diamond, I desire to believe that it does not;
> Let me not become attached to beliefs I may not want.
> — the Litany of Tarski
