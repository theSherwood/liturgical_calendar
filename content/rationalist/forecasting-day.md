## Meaning

A quiet discipline hiding inside New Year's Day: not vague resolutions but real, falsifiable predictions with probabilities attached. The forecasting research of Philip Tetlock showed that ordinary people who track their guesses and score themselves honestly can become genuinely, measurably better at seeing the future than credentialed pundits who never keep score. The whole trick is keeping score.

## Observance

Two rituals, one sitting. First, open last year's sealed predictions and grade them — celebrate the honest misses as much as the hits. Then each person writes a handful of concrete predictions for the year ahead (personal, worldly, silly) and, crucially, a probability on each. Seal them for next New Year.
