## Meaning

A companion to Petrov Day, and if anything an even closer call. On 27 October 1962 — the tensest day of the Cuban Missile Crisis — a Soviet submarine, out of contact and being shaken by American depth charges, believed war had already broken out. Its captain moved to fire a nuclear torpedo. By protocol, all three senior officers aboard had to agree. Two said yes. Vasili Arkhipov said no, and held the line until the boat surfaced. His single refusal may have prevented the Third World War.

## Observance

Keep it the way we keep Petrov Day: quietly, and with attention. Read Arkhipov's story aloud. Note that the world has now been saved, that we know of, at least twice — both times by one calm person doubting the alarm. Ask what it takes to be the lone 'no' in a room, and whether we could be.
