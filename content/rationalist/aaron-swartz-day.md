## Meaning

A day both to celebrate and to mourn. Aaron Swartz helped build the modern web — RSS, Creative Commons, the early Reddit — before he was twenty, and spent his short life fighting to make human knowledge free to everyone. Facing an aggressive federal prosecution for downloading academic papers he believed should be public, he took his own life at twenty-six. He is a reminder of how much one young person can build, and how badly the world can treat the people trying hardest to improve it.

## Observance

Read something that's normally locked away, and support the things that keep knowledge open — a library, Wikipedia, an open-access archive. Talk about knowledge as a common inheritance, not a product, and about being gentle with the intense young people who are trying to fix things.

## Reading

> "Information is power. But like all power, there are those who want to keep it for themselves… Sharing isn't immoral — it's a moral imperative."
> — Aaron Swartz, Guerilla Open Access Manifesto
