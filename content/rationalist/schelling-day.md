## Meaning

Thomas Schelling asked: if you had to meet a stranger in a city tomorrow with no way to arrange it, where would you go? Most people name the same place — the minds meet without a word. He called it a focal point, or 'Schelling point.' This day is ours: a fixed, known time that everyone can find without negotiating, to reconnect with the people we intend to keep close — so that closeness never depends on someone remembering to schedule it. And where better to hold it than Schelling's own birthday? Of all the dates in the year, it is the one you'd guess if you had to guess blind — which is the whole point, and the joke.

## Observance

Reach for the people who've drifted just out of orbit — the friend, the cousin, the grandparent you always mean to call. Because the day is fixed and known, no one has to organize it; you just show up, and trust that they're reaching back.

## Reading

> Most situations — perhaps every situation for people who are practiced at this kind of game — provide some clue for coordinating behavior, some focal point for each person's expectation of what the other expects him to expect to be expected to do. Finding the key, or rather finding a key — any key that is mutually recognized as the key becomes the key — may depend on imagination more than on logic.
> — Thomas C. Schelling, The Strategy of Conflict (Harvard University Press, 1960), p. 57
