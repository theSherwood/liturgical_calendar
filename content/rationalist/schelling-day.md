## Meaning

Thomas Schelling asked: if you had to meet a stranger in a city tomorrow with no way to arrange it, where would you go? Most people name the same place — the minds meet without a word. He called it a focal point, or 'Schelling point.' This day is ours: a fixed, known time that everyone can find without negotiating, to reconnect with the people we intend to keep close — so that closeness never depends on someone remembering to schedule it.

## Observance

Reach for the people who've drifted just out of orbit — the friend, the cousin, the grandparent you always mean to call. Because the day is fixed and known, no one has to organize it; you just show up, and trust that they're reaching back.

## Reading

Schelling's puzzle: strangers told to meet in New York, unable to communicate, mostly choose the same spot — Grand Central, at noon. Coordination without a word.

> "People can often concert their intentions or expectations with others if each knows that the other is trying to do the same."
> — Thomas Schelling, The Strategy of Conflict

So we pick a point on the year and agree, in advance and forever, to meet there.
