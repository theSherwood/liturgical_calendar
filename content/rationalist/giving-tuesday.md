## Meaning

The calendar's turn from gratitude to generosity. After Thanksgiving's feast and the shopping days that follow it, one day set aside for giving — and, in our family, for giving *well*. Not all help helps equally; some gifts do a hundred times the good of others for the same cost. Taking that seriously is its own kind of love.

## Observance

As a family, choose somewhere to give and do it together today. Look up how much good the gift actually does — lives improved per dollar, not warm feelings per dollar. Let the children pick a share. Make generosity a thing we practice on purpose, not just feel.

## Reading

> "If it is in our power to prevent something bad from happening, without thereby sacrificing anything of comparable moral importance, we ought, morally, to do it."
> — Peter Singer, "Famine, Affluence, and Morality"

The stranger drowning in the shallow pond, whom you would ruin your clothes to save without a second thought, is not made less real by distance. This day asks a plain question: with what we can spare, how much good can we actually do — and then, will we?
