## Meaning

Athens' great birthday for itself — a procession up to the Acropolis, athletic and musical contests, and the whole city celebrating what it was. Beneath the religion, it's a festival of civic pride: the idea that belonging to a place, and helping build it, is worth a party.

## Observance

Celebrate your own city or town. Take a walk to a landmark you're proud of, learn one thing about your place's history, and talk about what makes it worth belonging to.

## Reading

> "Sing, goddess, of the wrath of Achilles, son of Peleus, that ruinous wrath that brought countless sorrows upon the Achaeans."
> — Homer, the opening of the Iliad, recited at the Panathenaia

Athens' great birthday for itself: a procession up to the Acropolis, athletic and musical contests, and — every four years — the whole of Homer performed aloud, in order, over days. Celebrate your own city as the Athenians did theirs.
