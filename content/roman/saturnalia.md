## Meaning

The wildest party of the Roman year and the direct ancestor of Christmas: a week when schools closed, gifts were exchanged, gambling was legal, and — most famously — masters served their slaves at table. Saturnalia's genius was letting the whole social order relax for a week so it didn't snap.

## Observance

Adopt one Saturnalian rule for a day: the children give the orders, or the parents do the chores. Small gifts (candles and figurines were traditional), a big shared meal, and the greeting the Romans shouted — 'Io Saturnalia!'
