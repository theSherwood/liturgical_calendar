## Meaning

By tradition, Romulus founded Rome on this day in 753 BC — and the Romans celebrated it every year as the birthday of their city, layered onto the older shepherds' festival of the Parilia. From a village on seven hills grew an empire that gave the West its law, its roads, its calendar, its languages, and half its imagination. Rome fell (twice, if you count Constantinople), but it never really left: we are still, in a hundred quiet ways, living in its long afternoon.

## Observance

Celebrate the birthday of a city 2,700-odd years old. Eat Roman — bread, olives, cheese, a little wine. Spot the Roman still around you: the months, the alphabet, the word 'senate,' the very idea of a republic. Raise a glass: 'Roma aeterna.'

## Reading

The empire ended, of course. But the roads, the laws, and the words outlived the legions — which is its own kind of empire without end.
