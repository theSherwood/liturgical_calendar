## Meaning

One of Rome's oldest and strangest festivals — a wild rite of purification and fertility at winter's end, and a distant, disreputable ancestor of Valentine's Day. It marks the moment the year starts leaning toward spring and life.

## Observance

A light-hearted echo the day after Valentine's: a brisk walk or run outdoors to 'shake off winter,' and a spring-cleaning of one room to purify the house for the coming season.
